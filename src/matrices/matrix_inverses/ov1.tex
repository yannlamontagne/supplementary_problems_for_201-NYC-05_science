Consider $p(X)=X^3-2X^2+4X-3I$
\Question If $p(A)=\begin{mat}2 & 1\\ -1 & 2\end{mat}$, compute $p(\trans{A})$
\Question If $p(B)=0$ where $B$ is an $\nbyn{n}$ matrix, find $B^{-1}$ in terms of $B$. 
\end{Exercise}

\begin{Answer}
\Question
$
p(\trans{A})=
\begin{mat}
2 & -1\\
1 & 2
\end{mat}
$
\Question $B^{-1}=\frac13(B^2-2B+4I)$

\end{Answer}
